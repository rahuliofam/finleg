Foundation (Austin-Specific)

Austin sits on expansive clay soils that shrink and swell with moisture changes. This is the #1 cause of foundation problems in Central Texas.

What to Look For During Tour:

  • Cracks in interior/exterior walls (especially diagonal)
  • Doors and windows that stick or won't close
  • Uneven or sloping floors
  • Gaps between walls and ceiling/floor
  • Cracks in foundation visible from outside
  • If pier & beam: check crawl space for moisture

Recommended Inspections:

  • Structural engineer evaluation ($300-$500)
  • Check for previous foundation work (permits)

Plumbing (Critical for 1948)

1948 homes typically have galvanized steel water supply lines and cast iron drain lines. Both degrade over time.

Key Risks:

  • Steel pipes corrode internally → low pressure, rusty water
  • Cast iron drains crack → sewer leaks under slab
  • Tree roots can infiltrate damaged pipes
  • Under-slab repairs: $25,000-$40,000
  • Repiping entire house: $8,000-$15,000 (PEX)

Recommended Inspections:

  • Hydrostatic (static) plumbing test
  • Sewer camera scope ($150-$300)
  • Check water pressure at all fixtures
