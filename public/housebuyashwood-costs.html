Total permitting budget (fees + professionals): $9,500 – $16,500. This is the "soft cost" investment required before construction begins. The professional services are mandatory — Austin requires certified structural plans and a boundary survey for this type of project.

Review Types Required

Residential Plan Review (RPR)

Submit digital plans through Austin Build + Connect (AB+C) portal. Must include: certified boundary survey, site plan with impervious cover, floor plans for both levels, and Setback Plane (McMansion) analysis.

Historic Preservation Review

HPO reviews impact on historical integrity. Design tip: match the 1948 stucco finish and roof pitch to get administrative approval and avoid a full Historic Landmark Commission hearing.

Environmental / Tree Review

Tree survey required. Protected trees (19"+ diameter) and Heritage trees (24"+) trigger Critical Root Zone analysis. Construction equipment access must avoid root zones.

Trade Permits (EP, PP, MP)

Electrical: new sub-panel likely needed. Plumbing: check if 1948 clay/cast-iron sewer lateral needs replacement. Mechanical: mini split must meet 2024 IECC.

Structural Engineering Challenges

1948 Foundation Risk: Most garages from this era have unreinforced 4-inch concrete slabs. A structural engineer must perform a slab-depth test before permitting. If the foundation is insufficient, underpinning could add $15,000–$30,000 to the project.

Strategic Recommendations

1. Get Surveys First
Boundary + tree survey to map Critical Root Zones against garage footprint. Structural engineer slab-depth test to confirm the 1948 foundation can handle a 2nd story. This determines if the project is viable at your budget.
2. Design Within NCCD Limits
Keep under 25.5 ft accessory height to avoid Board of Adjustment variance ($1,000 fee + major delays). Stay within the Setback Plane. Consider the "Preservation Bonus" under HOME initiative for FAR exemptions.
3. Engage Historic Preservation Early
Present a design that matches the 1948 stucco/wood-siding palette. Avoid modern industrial materials (corrugated metal). Goal: administrative approval to skip the full Historic Landmark Commission hearing.
4. Schedule a Preliminary Plan Review (PPR)
Meet with city staff before filing to identify deal-breakers (heritage tree conflicts, NCCD violations). This prevents costly comment cycles and re-submissions.
5. Budget for Soft Costs
~$5K city fees + $7K–$12K professionals (architect, structural engineer, surveyor). The professional docs are mandatory for permit approval in a historic area like Delwood.
Do NOT skip permits. Unpermitted work in Austin carries a fine of triple the permit cost or $25/day (whichever is greater). Unpermitted structures can receive a Stop Work Order and may be required to be demolished if they can't meet modern structural and fire codes.
ROI context: A permitted garage conversion in Austin delivers up to 80% ROI. In Cherrywood/Delwood, ADUs rent for $1,500–$2,000/mo. The $9.5K–$16.5K permitting investment is recovered in 5–8 months of ADU rental income.
